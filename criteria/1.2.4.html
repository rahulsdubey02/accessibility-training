1.2.4 Captions (Live)

Captions must be provided for all live audio content in synchronised media — webinars, live streams, broadcast events, and other real-time video with audio. The captions must be synchronised and provided in real time.

Intent

Live captions allow deaf and hard-of-hearing users to participate in real-time events on equal terms. Unlike prerecorded content (SC 1.2.2), live captions cannot be prepared in advance and must be produced in real time. Two main approaches exist:

  • CART (Communication Access Realtime Translation) — a trained human stenographer types at 200+ wpm using a specialised steno keyboard. Gold-standard accuracy (~98%); includes speaker IDs and sound effects. Most expensive.
  • ASR (Automatic Speech Recognition) — real-time machine captioning (Google Meet, Teams, Zoom auto-captions). Faster to deploy and free/low cost, but significantly lower accuracy — especially for technical terms and accents. WCAG accepts ASR as a live captioning method, though CART is strongly preferred.

Post-event captions added after the stream ends do not satisfy this criterion — they are prerecorded captions (SC 1.2.2) for what is now a recording. Live captions must be available during the live event.

Key Distinctions

Live Captions Have Inherent Latency
Real-time captions always have a short delay — CART typically 1–3 seconds, ASR up to 5+ seconds. This is accepted by WCAG. However, captions added only as a post-event archive are not acceptable as live captions.
Applies to Live Synchronised Media Only
This criterion covers live video with audio. A live audio-only stream is covered by SC 1.2.9 (AAA). A live text chat or live transcript alongside audio does not constitute "synchronised media" and is a different kind of accessible alternative.
Accuracy Expectations Are Lower for Live
WCAG acknowledges that live captions are harder to produce accurately than prerecorded ones. The requirement is for "real-time captions" — not perfect captions. However, accuracy should still be as high as reasonably achievable.

Example 2 of 4

ASR Auto-captions — Acceptable but With Caveats

A small team holds a weekly internal video call using Zoom's built-in auto-captions. The speaker is a native English speaker with a neutral accent discussing everyday topics. ASR accuracy is high enough for this context. However, the team should be aware of accuracy limitations for technical or multilingual content.

Guideline: ASR auto-captions in Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet satisfy 1.2.4 for general-purpose meetings with clear speakers. For technical or specialist content, book CART. Always offer to provide CART on request as reasonable adjustment.

Example 3 of 4

Pre-scripted Live Events — Preparing Captions in Advance

A news organisation broadcasts a live speech by a public official. The speech text is known in advance. The captioning team loads the prepared text, and the captioner advances through the pre-written captions in sync with the speaker, making corrections for any deviations in real time.
